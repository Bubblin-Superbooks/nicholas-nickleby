Chapter 14

H_aving the Misfortune to treat of none but Common People, is necessarily of a Mean and Vulgar Character_

In that quarter of London in which Golden Square is situated, there is a bygone, faded, tumble-down street, with two irregular rows of tall meagre houses, which seem to have stared each other out of countenance years ago. The very chimneys appear to have grown dismal and melancholy, from having had nothing better to look at than the chimneys over the way.
